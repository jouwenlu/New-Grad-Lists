Resources

Our team has put together some key resources to help you navigate the recruiting process. Everything here is what we have used ourselves, and we truly recommend everything on this page.

Websites and Subscriptions

We all appreciate simple things, and these resources are great starts before diving in deeper.
Mergers and Inquisitions (M&I)
This is a reliable blog that is kept up to date. The author of the articles on this website, Brian, is knowledgeable about all things investment banking and lays out information with a great structure. Our team recommends this website to learn about the basics; some good reads include “How to Get into Investment Banking” and “Write a Resume or Cover Letter.”
Morning Brew
Since we’re busy students ourselves, we love Morning Brew and the convenience it offers. It sends daily updates on financial news and the markets straight to your inbox. The content is interesting to read about, and this newsletter helps you stay up to date on the markets during interview season.

Interview Preparation

Great for practicing sample questions you might be asked during an interview.
WSO Technical Guide
This guide covers the basics and contains questions that banks most frequently ask. The WSO guide also offers actual past interview questions and is especially helpful for S&T or capital markets interviews (stocks, bonds, loans, interest rates, currencies, options, and derivatives).
M&I Guide
The M&I guide is a mandatory resource that helps prepare candidates for technical interviews. It can be more in depth (in the “Advanced” sections) than the WSO guide, but we still recommend that you read this to be as prepared as possible because we find that candidates sometimes hear these questions verbatim.
WSO Behavioral Guide
This guide gives you the gold standard for how to answer behavioral interview questions, as well as strategies for structuring your responses. Behavioral interviews are the most important segment of the process because most candidates answer technical questions correctly and pass the bar, so reading this guide gives you the opportunity to differentiate yourself.
WSO Networking Guide
Last but not least, the networking guide is especially important for folks who do not have on campus recruitment at their universities. It gives you a list of questions to ask so that you can always be prepared for professional conversations. Some other information here include LinkedIn strategies, cover letter formatting, and business etiquette.

Books

Some are technical, and some are essential or interesting books relating to finance that can benefit your career in the long run.
Investment Banking: Valuation, Leveraged Buyouts, and Mergers and Acquisitions
Think of this as a more in depth M&I guide. EB’s (or M&A groups at some MM or BB banks) can ask very technical questions, and reading this book is one way to differentiate yourself.
Distressed Debt Analysis: Strategies for Speculative Investors
If you’re interested in Financial Restructuring (rx) groups, this book provides the technical knowledge that is necessary to perform well in interviews.
The Intelligent Investor: The Definitive Book on Value Investing
This is a must have for anyone interested in a career in financial services. The resource is not only helpful for investment banking, but also for the buy side.
Liar's Poker
A classic. It’s an interesting and fun read that gives you a sense of what Wall Street was like (as a trader).

Communities

Learn from other folks on Wall Street about recruiting and life in general as a banker.
Wall Street Oasis
This is a must visit. The forum offers the most up to date conversations and by far has the largest number of people on Wall Street on its platform. It also provides interview guides that we strongly recommend.
Reddit/FinancialCareers
Reddit/FinancialCareers is an underrated resource for information on the ins and outs of banking. It seems that users here provide a more diverse perspective on the pros and cons of investment banking compared to WSO.
